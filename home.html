Werner Heisenberg

one of the main pioneers of the theory of quantum mechanics.

Here's a time line of theoretical physicist Werner Heisenberg's life:

  • 1901 - Born in Würzburg, Kingdom of Bavaria, German Empire.
  • 1920 - went to the Maximilian school at Munich
  • 1922-1923 - he went to Göttingen to study physics under Max Born, Franck, and Hilbert.
  • 1923 - he took his Ph.D. at the University of Munich and then became Assistant to Max Born at the University of Göttingen.
  • 1924 - he worked, with a Rockefeller Grant, with Niels Bohr, at the University of Copenhagen, returning for the summer of 1925 to Göttingen. he gained the venia legendi [1] at that University.
  • 1925 - Heisenberg’s name will always be associated with his theory of quantum mechanics, when he was only 23 years old. For this theory and the applications of it which resulted especially in the discovery of allotropic forms of hydrogen.
  • 1926 - he was appointed Lecturer in Theoretical Physics at the University of Copenhagen under Niels Bohr
  • 1927 - when he was only 26, he was appointed Professor of Theoretical Physics at the University of Leipzig.
  • 1929 - he went on a lecture tour to the United States, Japan, and India.
  • 1932 - Heisenberg was awarded the Nobel Prize for Physics.
  • 1937 - Heisenberg married Elisabeth Schumacher. They have seven children, and live in Munich.
  • 1941 - he was appointed Professor of Physics at the University of Berlin and Director of the Kaiser Wilhelm Institute for Physics there.
  • 1945 - At the end of the Second World War he, and other German physicists, were taken prisoner by American troops and sent to England
  • 1946 - he returned to Germany and reorganized, with his colleagues, the Institute for Physics at Göttingen.
  • 1948 - stayed for some months in Cambridge, England, to give lectures, and in 1950 and 1954 he was invited to lecture in the United States.
  • 1955-1956 - he gave the Gifford Lectures at the University of St. Andrews, Scotland, these lectures being subsequently published as a book.
  • 1957 - Heisenberg was interested in work on problems of plasma physics and thermonuclear processes, and also much work in close collaboration with the International Institute of Atomic Physics at Geneva. He was for several years Chairman of the Scientific Policy Committee of this Institute and subsequently remained a member of this Committee.
  • 1976 - died at the age of 74 in Munich, Bavaria, West Germany
